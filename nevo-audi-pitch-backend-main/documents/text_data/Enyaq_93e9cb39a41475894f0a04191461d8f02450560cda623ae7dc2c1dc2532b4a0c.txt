Recovery of braking energy During regenerative braking, the kinetic energy of the vehicle is converted into electricity, which is then stored in the high-voltage battery. In this way, energy is used efficiently, and the range of the vehicle is increased by partially charging the battery during driving. Regenerative braking occurs in various driving situations and features multiple modes that can be changed at any time. The intensity of the regenerative braking force can be manually adjusted by the driver or automatically by the vehicle's control unit.
